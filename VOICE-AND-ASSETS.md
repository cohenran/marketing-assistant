# Recording your voice & assets

The generator copies *your* tone and suggests *your real* screenshots. Spend 20 minutes
filling `voice.txt` and `assets.txt` once — every future draft gets better.

---

## Prompt: capture your brand voice → paste answers into `voice.txt`

Answer these in your own words (or just paste real things you've written):

1. **Show, don't tell.** Paste 2-3 things you've actually written that sound like you — a
   tweet, a text to a friend, a payeragraph from anywhere. This is the single best input.
2. **Describe your tone** in 3-5 adjectives (e.g. blunt, funny, warm, no-BS, nerdy).
3. **Sentence style** — short and punchy, or longer and thoughtful?
4. **Words/phrases you love** and ones you'd **never** use (e.g. no "revolutionize",
   no "game-changer", no emoji — or the opposite).
5. **How do you talk about competitors / swiping?** (playful, respectful, savage?)
6. **One line you'd genuinely write** about why you built the app.

Paste the answers into `voice.txt`. Real examples beat descriptions — include at least one.

---

## Prompt: list your visual assets → paste into `assets.txt`

For each item write one line: `type: short description`. Capture these once:

- **The hero feature** — screenshot of the 4-match limit / lock screen.
- **Proof it works** — a real (blurred) conversation or a date that happened.
- **Onboarding** — a 15-30s screen recording of picking your 4.
- **The contrast** — a visual of "hundreds of swipes → 4" (gif/graphic).
- **You** — a founder photo for personal/LinkedIn posts.
- **App Store** — your best 2-3 app screenshots.

Write them so the suggestion is actionable, e.g.:
```
screenshot: "4 matches" home screen
video (15s): onboarding, choosing your 4
photo: founder headshot
```

The generator ends each draft with `SUGGESTED VISUAL: ...`, picking from this list. If the
list is empty it tells you what to capture instead.

---

## Reminder

The app **generates** drafts only. You **edit** them and **post** them yourself, manually.
Nothing is auto-published anywhere.
